A TRIBUTE TO NELSON ROLIHLAHLA MANDELA

"A great leader and president who fought for freedom and justice for South Africans and Africans as a whole."

Things Nelson Mandela did during his lifetime

1. After 27 years in prison, Nelson Mandela was freed in 1990 and negotiated with State President F. W. de Klerk the end of apartheid in South Africa, bringing peace to a racially divided country and leading the fight for human rights around the world. All human beings are born free and equal in dignity and rights.

2. He won the Nobel Prize for Peace in 1993, along with South Africa's president at the time, F.W. de Klerk, for having led the transition from apartheid to a multiracial democracy. Mandela is also known for being the first black president of South Africa, serving from 1994 to 1999.

3. Mandela and his fellow members of the ANC used non-violent tactics like strikes and demonstrations to protest apartheid.

5. Nelson Mandela's staunch efforts against apartheid, racism and social injustice rightly radiate far beyond the continent of Africa. He is a global icon, among other things because he showed that hatred cannot be overcome by hatred but by empathy.

6. Nelson Mandela established the Truth and Reconciliation Commission, providing a safe place to investigate past human rights abuses, avoiding civil war and bloodshed. Nelson Mandela established measures to combat poverty and expand healthcare services.

7. Nelson Mandela was a protester, prisoner, and peacemaker. He was a hero for South African non-whites by getting rid of the apartheid system. He is also a heroic figure to others for achieving what had accomplished. Mandela was born in Transkei, South Africa on July 18th, 1918.

8. He became an ambassador for peace.

9. He stood his ground to make sure Africans are no longer treated as slaves to the Whites.
